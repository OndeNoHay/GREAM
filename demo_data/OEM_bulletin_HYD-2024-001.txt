SUBJECT: Replacement of Hydraulic System Pressure Regulator Valve (PRV)
         Part Number: HYD-PRV-3A (superseded by HYD-PRV-4B)

1. REASON

Hydraulic pressure oscillations (±200 PSI above normal system pressure of 3000 PSI)
have been reported in service. Root cause analysis identified premature wear on the
internal seat of the Pressure Regulator Valve (PRV), Part Number HYD-PRV-3A.
A redesigned valve (HYD-PRV-4B) with an improved Inconel 718 seat is available
as a direct replacement.

Failure mode: if the PRV seat degrades beyond 0.08 mm wear, system pressure can
exceed 3400 PSI, potentially causing hydraulic hose failures and landing gear
retraction anomalies.

2. DESCRIPTION OF WORK

The maintenance task requires removal and replacement of the hydraulic PRV located
on the hydraulic power pack assembly (Access Panel HYD-20). The procedure also
includes a functional test of the hydraulic system at operating pressure.

Estimated maintenance time: 2.5 man-hours.
Special tooling required: pressure gauge (0–5000 PSI), hydraulic fluid
collection equipment, torque wrench (0–50 Nm).

3. PARTS REQUIRED

Item  Part Number    Description                         Qty  Unit
1     HYD-PRV-4B     Pressure Regulator Valve (revised)   1   EA
2     OR-2345-V      O-Ring, Viton 2.5 mm                 2   EA
3     HYD-F-BLUE     Hydraulic Fluid (Mil-PRF-5606)       1   QT (as req'd)

Old PRV (HYD-PRV-3A) must be returned to the manufacturer for analysis.

4. ACCOMPLISHMENT INSTRUCTIONS

4.1 WARNINGS
WARNING: Depressurize the hydraulic system to 0 PSI before starting work.
         Failure to depressurize can cause hydraulic fluid injection injury.
WARNING: Hydraulic fluid is flammable. Keep away from ignition sources.

4.2 CAUTIONS
CAUTION: Do not overtighten the PRV mounting bolts. Apply torque to 25 Nm ±2 Nm.
CAUTION: Ensure all hydraulic fittings are clean and free of debris before installation.

4.3 PROCEDURE SUMMARY
Step 1: Depressurize the hydraulic system. Verify system pressure = 0 PSI.
Step 2: Open Access Panel HYD-20 on the left side of the fuselage.
Step 3: Disconnect hydraulic lines from the PRV. Cap all open ports immediately.
Step 4: Remove PRV mounting bolts (4x M8). Remove PRV HYD-PRV-3A.
Step 5: Inspect PRV mounting interface for damage. Clean seating surface.
Step 6: Install new O-rings (OR-2345-V) on replacement PRV HYD-PRV-4B.
Step 7: Install PRV HYD-PRV-4B. Torque mounting bolts to 25 Nm ±2 Nm.
Step 8: Connect hydraulic lines. Torque hydraulic fittings per AMM 29-10-00.
Step 9: Restore hydraulic system pressure. Perform functional test per AMM 29-00-00.
Step 10: Verify system pressure = 3000 PSI ±100 PSI under normal load.
Step 11: Check for leaks at all connections. No leakage permitted.
Step 12: Close Access Panel HYD-20. Make appropriate maintenance record entry.

5. TEST AND VERIFICATION

After installation, perform the Hydraulic System Operational Test:
- Pressurize system to 3000 PSI nominal
- Cycle landing gear: 3 complete retraction/extension cycles
- Verify PRV maintains pressure within 2900–3100 PSI throughout cycling
- Verify no hydraulic fluid leakage at PRV or connected lines
